Format Screws

Articles that deviate from the standard format in odd or unique ways are referred to as format screws. Often, this change in format is vital in some way to presenting the object that the article is about. For a minor example, SCP-426 is a toaster that can only be referred to in the first person, and as such, the entire article is written seemingly from the perspective of the toaster. More major examples of format screws may sometimes include hidden collapsibles, invisible text, or different versions of the same article.
