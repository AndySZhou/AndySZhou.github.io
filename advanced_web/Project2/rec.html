Recommendations

With that out of the way, it’s time for some recommendations. The following SCPs are a mix of good starting points, highly regarded articles, and some of my personal favorites. I will also include some recommendations for canons and SCP-001 proposals.

Note: Lower-numbered articles are generally articles that were written earlier in the site’s history, and as a result, may generally serve as better starting points than higher-numbered articles.

Starting Points:

These articles can ease you into reading the SCP Wiki in addition to being compelling reads.

SCP-173



by Moto42 AKA The USS Walrus

As the literal starting point for the entire SCP Wiki, SCP-173, was the original article posted to the /x/ board, long before the current site had even been created. By modern SCP standards, it could be seen as somewhat boring, but it has the basic format and set the tone for the rest of the SCP Wiki.



SCP-682



by Dr.Gears

SCP-682 is maybe one of the other most well-known SCPs besides 173, and one of the staples of early SCP articles. It can serve as a good example of the threats that Keter-class objects can pose, and also heavily features crosstesting, where the Foundation uses SCPs to test another SCP. Crosstesting is looked down upon by current site standards, but you may find other examples of it in some other early articles.



SCP-087



by Zaeyde

SCP-087 is one of the more overtly horror-focused objects on this list. It serves as a good introduction how exploration logs are used in articles, and maintains a good sense of mounting dread as the article progresses. It also has a very creepy picture if you scroll down.



SCP-1000



by thedeadlymoose

SCP-1000 presents a twist on a common urban myth, taking a familiar concept and fitting it into the SCP world. It also provides a good example of the Foundation limiting information based on clearance level, a concept that is used in several other articles.



SCP-2316



by djkaktus

SCP-2316 presents a good example of a cognitohazardous SCP, along with minor format screws and meta aspects. (Hint: Highlight parts of the article where it looks like there’s maybe too much white space…)



Well-Known Articles:

These articles are well-regarded in the site community, and many of them are popular outside of it

Note: the “1000” articles (ex. SCP-1000, SCP-2000, etc.), are articles that won writing contests for their slot, and are generally highly regarded.

SCP-093


Art by alexandreev on DeviantArt

by NekoChris

An early staple of the SCP Wiki, SCP-093 is a hugely influential article, having gained fame even outside of the site in the form of artwork and fan games. Considered “ahead of it’s time” for a Series 1 article, it is a very long read but succeeds in creating a unique sense of horror. There is also an SCP Declassified on it here.



SCP-096



by DrEverettMann

Similar in concept to SCP-173, but very different in execution, with a supplementary document fleshing out the full horror of the object. In addition to information about the object, the article also features some good character building.



SCP-106



by Dr.Gears

One of the more uniquely scary humanoid SCPs, with some horrifying pictures and a bunch of excellent tales attached to it. Note: This SCP and its accompanying tales are particularaly gruesome, even by SCP standards, so proceed with caution.



SCP-2935



by djkaktus

Makes heavy use of exploration logs, but creates an excellent sense of dread, with good characterization. There is also an SCP Declassified on it here.



Personal Favorites:

A blanket category for some of my personal favorite mainlist articles, 001 Proposals, and Canons.

SCP-1730



by djkaktus

Very long, and heavily based on exploration logs, but very well written and maintains a strong sense of horror throughout.



SCP-2740



by djkaktus

Contrasting to some of the other items on this list, this article invokes a more dreamlike, uneasy sense of dread.



SCP-3000



by djkaktus (you may be recognizing a trend)

Long and character-driven, with a sense of existential horror.



SCP-3333



by Jekeled

Long and based in exploration logs.



SCP-001 Proposals:

The Factory

by Bright

One of the 001 Proposals that takes the form of a tale as opposed to an article. Presents a well-written and compelling tale of the Foundation’s origin, framed contextually as an O5 Council member telling the story from his point of view.



Past and Future

by Kalinin

Tells a story in many parts of how the Foundation ends. Has a uniquely horrifying concept and disturbing imagery. Is also very long.



Past and Future



by djkaktus and TwistedGears

In actuality, this is four SCP-001 proposals in one. The first three, “The Children”, “The Broken God”, and “Atonement”, all deal with different objects and could be seen as separate proposals, but “The Way It Ends”, the fourth and final part, connects them all in interesting ways, and culminates with a unique sense of existential dread. In particular, “The Way It All Ends” is my favorite 001 Proposal, and is extremely well-written all the way through, with good characterization and a unique spin on the creation of the Foundation and the Chaos Insurgency. These proposals are all also fairly long, with the fourth part being extremely long, so be warned.



Canons:

Resurrection Hub



Various authors

A series of tales set in a universe where the Foundation is attempting to make a task force using certain sentient SCP objects. Resurrection is notable for having a much brighter and more humorous tone than many other SCP canons, more similar in tone to something like a Marvel movie than, say, something by Stephen King, although the horror aspects are still present. Warning: this canon is pretty reliant on context from older Foundation “canon”, from before the “no set canon” rule.

A list of relevant articles and tales can be found at the bottom of the page, in the “Reading” tab, but the “Overview” tab can also serve as a good background-setter if you want to just jump right in.



Broken Masquerade Hub



Various authors

This canon deals with the aftermath of anomalies becoming public knowledge, and the Foundation becoming a public organization. It has a unique perspective, as the rest of the site is based in the fact that anomalies, and the Foundation, are kept secret from the public. As such, this canon explores themes and ideas that can’t be explored in other canons.
